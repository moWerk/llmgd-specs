# LLMGD — Large Language Model Governance Disclosure

A Creative-Commons-style label that says, honestly and verifiably, how much
LLM involvement a piece of work contains.

## Why

Somewhere between "fully hand-written" and "the machine did everything" lives
almost all modern work, and we have no vocabulary for the in-between. A
co-author trailer is a blunt instrument: it confuses blame with involvement.
A checkbox that says "AI-assisted" says nothing at all.

LLMGD gives the in-between a name. Two graded axes, a handful of evidence
flags, and one line in your commit or PR that anyone can read and any LLM can
verify against the session transcript that produced the work.

The idea sparked while shipping a watch app PR where every design decision
and every sentence was mine, but the typing was not. Not disclosing that felt
wrong. Disclosing it as co-authorship felt wrong too. So: a standard.

## The short version

- **Origin (O0–O4)**: who made the decisions the bytes express. O0 is
  machine-decided from bare prompts, O2 is human-designed and
  machine-expressed, O4 is untouched by any model.
- **Assurance flags (R, U, T, X)**: what verification happened before
  publication. Read in full, Understanding demonstrated, Tested against
  reality, eXternally reviewed.
- **Presets LLMGD-0 to LLMGD-5**: common combinations, the way CC-BY-SA is a
  preset over CC's building blocks.
- **Machine-gradeable**: the verdict is generated by a standardized prompt
  ([GRADING_PROMPT.md](GRADING_PROMPT.md)) run over the interaction
  transcript. Claims without transcript evidence grade down. Self-flattery
  loses to the rubric.

Full specification with all definitions and the reasoning behind them:
[SPEC.md](SPEC.md).

## Using it

Add the two lines to your commit, PR or document footer:

```
Disclosure: LLMGD-4 (human-designed, LLM-expressed; read, understood, device-tested)
LLMGD: v0.1; origin=O2; assurance=R,U,T; scope=code+messages; graded-by=<model>; retrieval=author-side
```

Then, ideally, publish the graded verdict or keep the transcript retrievable
for third-party grading. That is the whole standard.

## This repository's own label

Dogfood starts at home:

```
Disclosure: LLMGD-2 (human concept and constraints, LLM-designed detail and text; read, understood)
LLMGD: v0.1; origin=O1; assurance=R,U; scope=docs; graded-by=claude-fable-5; retrieval=author-side
```
